Brief Bio: I am going to join the School of Computer and Cyber Sciences at Augusta University this summer as an Assistant Professor. Before joining Augusta University, I am now working as a Postdoctoral Research Scholar in the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at Vanderbilt University (Sep 2016 – Jul 2019), and worked as a Postdoctoral Research Associate in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Texas at Arlington (Nov 2015 – Aug 2016), a Research Associate in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at the University of Hong Kong (May 2015 – Oct 2015), and briefly in the School of Transportation and Logistics at Southwest Jiaotong University as an Associate Professor (Jun 2014 – May 2015). I completed my Ph.D. at Southwest Jiaotong University in 2014 with a Ph.D. thesis on formal methods for hybrid traffic systems awarded the Outstanding Ph.D. Dissertation of Southwest Jiaotong University 2014.

You can find my CV here.

Research: My research interest is developing formal synthesis and verification techniques and software tools for cyber-physical systems (CPS). My current research centers on formal methods on safety, security and reliablity of learning-enabled CPS. I am also broadly interested in methods and applications across CPS domains, such as control synthesis, stability analysis, reachable set computation, hybrid systems, power and energy, transportation, fuzzy logic, and neural networks.
